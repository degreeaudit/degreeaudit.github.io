How to Type the Degree Symbol in iPhone Safari Address Bar

Home / Blog / Blog Details

In today’s fast-paced digital world, knowing how to efficiently use your iPhone for everyday tasks can save you time and frustration. One such task is typing special characters like the degree symbol (°) in the Safari address bar. Whether you’re checking the weather, researching climate change data, or simply sharing coordinates, the degree symbol is more relevant than ever.

Why the Degree Symbol Matters in 2024

Climate Change and Global Awareness

With rising global temperatures and extreme weather events dominating headlines, the degree symbol has become a critical part of discussions. Scientists, journalists, and activists frequently reference temperature changes (e.g., "1.5°C threshold") when discussing climate goals. Being able to quickly type this symbol ensures you stay engaged in these vital conversations.

Travel and Navigation

As international travel rebounds post-pandemic, people are increasingly using their iPhones to look up destinations, check local temperatures, or share GPS coordinates—all of which often require the degree symbol.

Methods to Type the Degree Symbol in Safari’s Address Bar

Using the iPhone Keyboard’s Hidden Features

  1. Open Safari and tap the address bar to bring up the keyboard.
  2. Switch to the numeric keyboard by tapping the "123" key.
  3. Press and hold the zero (0) key. A small pop-up will appear with the degree symbol (°).
  4. Slide your finger to select ° and release to insert it.

Copy-Pasting the Symbol

If the above method doesn’t work (depending on your iOS version or keyboard settings), you can:
1. Copy the degree symbol (°) from another source (e.g., a note or webpage).
2. Paste it directly into the Safari address bar.

Using Text Replacement (For Frequent Use)

For those who frequently need the symbol, setting up a text replacement shortcut can be a game-changer:
1. Go to Settings > General > Keyboard > Text Replacement.
2. Tap the "+" icon.
3. In the Phrase field, enter "°".
4. In the Shortcut field, type something easy to remember (e.g., "deg").
5. Now, typing "deg" in Safari will prompt the iPhone to replace it with "°".

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Keyboard Language Settings

If the degree symbol doesn’t appear when holding "0", ensure your keyboard is set to English (U.S.). Some language keyboards may not support this shortcut.

iOS Updates

Apple occasionally tweaks keyboard functionalities. If the symbol isn’t showing up, check for iOS updates under Settings > General > Software Update.

Broader Implications: Digital Literacy in a Warming World

The Role of Small Tech Skills

Seemingly minor tech skills—like typing a degree symbol—can enhance your ability to participate in global discussions. Whether you’re tweeting about record-breaking heatwaves or researching renewable energy projects, efficiency matters.

Not everyone knows these shortcuts, which can create barriers in communication. Sharing this knowledge helps bridge the digital divide, ensuring more people can contribute to critical conversations about our planet’s future.

Final Thoughts

Mastering small but powerful iPhone tricks—like typing the degree symbol—can make a big difference in how you interact with technology daily. In an era where climate data and global connectivity are paramount, these skills keep you informed, efficient, and ready to engage with the world’s most pressing issues.
