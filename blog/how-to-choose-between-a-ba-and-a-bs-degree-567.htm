How to Choose Between a BA and a BS Degree

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Choosing between a Bachelor of Arts (BA) and a Bachelor of Science (BS) degree is one of the most critical decisions students face in higher education. While both degrees hold value, they cater to different academic and career goals. In today’s rapidly evolving job market—shaped by AI, climate change, and global economic shifts—the choice between a BA and a BS can significantly impact your future opportunities.

Understanding the Core Differences

BA Degree: Breadth Over Depth

A Bachelor of Arts (BA) emphasizes a broad, liberal arts education. It encourages critical thinking, creativity, and interdisciplinary learning. Common BA majors include:
- English
- History
- Psychology
- Political Science

BA programs often require fewer technical courses, allowing students to explore electives in philosophy, sociology, or foreign languages. This flexibility is ideal for careers in law, media, or public policy—fields where communication and analytical skills are paramount.

BS Degree: Specialization and Technical Skills

A Bachelor of Science (BS) focuses on specialized, technical, or scientific training. BS programs typically include:
- Computer Science
- Engineering
- Biology
- Data Science

These degrees often require advanced coursework in math, lab sciences, or programming. With industries like AI and renewable energy booming, a BS can provide a competitive edge in STEM fields.

Key Factors to Consider

Career Goals and Industry Demand

The job market increasingly favors technical skills. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, STEM occupations are projected to grow 8% by 2030, faster than non-STEM fields. If you’re aiming for a career in software development, biomedical research, or environmental engineering, a BS may be the better choice.

However, BA degrees remain valuable in fields like:
- Journalism
- International Relations
- Marketing

For example, a BA in Communications could lead to roles in digital media, where storytelling and audience engagement are crucial.

Curriculum and Learning Style

  • BA: More writing, discussion-based classes, and humanities courses.
  • BS: More labs, problem-solving, and quantitative analysis.

If you thrive in collaborative, creative environments, a BA might suit you better. If you prefer structured, data-driven work, a BS could be ideal.

Graduate School and Further Education

Some graduate programs favor applicants with a BS. For instance:
- Medical schools often prefer BS degrees in Biology or Chemistry.
- MBA programs may accept BA graduates but value quantitative skills from a BS.

However, law schools and humanities-based PhD programs typically don’t discriminate between BA and BS degrees.

The Role of Emerging Global Trends

AI and Automation

As AI reshapes industries, both BA and BS graduates must adapt. A BS in Computer Science prepares you to build AI systems, while a BA in Ethics or Philosophy could position you to address AI’s societal impacts.

Climate Change and Sustainability

A BS in Environmental Science is directly applicable to green energy jobs. Meanwhile, a BA in Public Policy can lead to advocacy or regulatory roles in climate action.

Remote Work and Digital Nomadism

The rise of remote work means skills matter more than ever. A BA in Digital Marketing or a BS in Cybersecurity can both offer lucrative remote career paths.

Final Tips for Making Your Decision

  1. Research job postings in your desired field—do they prefer BA or BS graduates?
  2. Talk to professionals in your industry.
  3. Consider double majors or minors to blend technical and soft skills.

Ultimately, the best degree aligns with your passions, strengths, and the future you envision. Whether you choose a BA or BS, lifelong learning will be key to staying relevant in a fast-changing world.
