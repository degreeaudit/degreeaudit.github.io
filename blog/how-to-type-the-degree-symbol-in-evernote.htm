How to Type the Degree Symbol in Evernote

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Evernote is one of the most versatile note-taking apps available today, helping millions of users organize their thoughts, projects, and research. Whether you're a student, a professional, or just someone who loves jotting down ideas, knowing how to insert special characters like the degree symbol (°) can be incredibly useful—especially when discussing topics like climate change, scientific data, or even cooking recipes.

In this guide, we’ll explore multiple ways to type the degree symbol in Evernote across different devices, while also touching on why this tiny symbol plays a big role in today’s world.

Why the Degree Symbol Matters in 2024

The degree symbol isn’t just for weather reports anymore. With global temperatures rising and climate discussions dominating headlines, accurately representing data is crucial. Scientists, journalists, and activists rely on precise notation to communicate critical information about:

  • Global Warming – Recording temperature changes in reports and studies.
  • Renewable Energy – Measuring solar panel efficiency or wind turbine angles.
  • Health & Medicine – Documenting fever readings or lab results.

Even outside science, the degree symbol appears in cooking (oven temperatures), navigation (latitude/longitude), and engineering. Knowing how to insert it quickly can save time and improve clarity in your notes.

How to Type the Degree Symbol in Evernote

On Windows

If you're using Evernote on a Windows PC, you have several options:

1. Keyboard Shortcut

Press and hold Alt, then type 0176 on the numeric keypad. Release Alt, and the ° symbol will appear.

2. Character Map

  • Open the Character Map app (search for it in the Start menu).
  • Find the degree symbol, click Select, then Copy.
  • Paste it into Evernote.

3. AutoCorrect (If Using Evernote via Microsoft Word)

Set up an AutoCorrect rule in Word to replace "deg" with "°". Since Evernote supports pasting formatted text, this can be a quick workaround.

On Mac

Mac users can insert the degree symbol even faster:

1. Keyboard Shortcut

Press Option + Shift + 8.

2. Character Viewer

  • Go to Edit > Emoji & Symbols (or press Control + Command + Space).
  • Search for "degree" and double-click the symbol to insert it.

On Mobile (iOS & Android)

Typing special characters on smartphones can be tricky, but here’s how to do it:

1. iOS (iPhone/iPad)

  • Open the keyboard in Evernote.
  • Press and hold the 0 key until the degree symbol appears as an option.
  • Slide your finger to select it.

2. Android

  • Switch to the numeric keyboard (usually by pressing ?123).
  • Look for the ° symbol, often under = or #.

Using Evernote’s Built-in Features

Evernote doesn’t have a direct "insert symbol" button, but you can:
- Copy-Paste from a web search or another document.
- Use Text Expanders (like TextExpander or PhraseExpress) to automate symbol insertion.

The Bigger Picture: Why Precision Matters

In an era of misinformation, small details like the degree symbol can make a difference. Imagine a climate report missing the ° symbol—readers might confuse Celsius with Fahrenheit, leading to misunderstandings.

Climate Data Accuracy

Organizations like the IPCC rely on precise notation. A single missing symbol could alter the interpretation of critical data.

Educational Notes

Students taking notes on chemistry or physics need correct symbols to avoid errors in formulas (e.g., 45°C vs. 45C).

Professional Communication

Engineers, chefs, and researchers depend on accurate symbols for clear communication.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Sometimes, the degree symbol doesn’t appear correctly. Here’s how to fix it:

Font Compatibility

If the symbol shows as a box, change Evernote’s font to a Unicode-supported one like Arial or Times New Roman.

Keyboard Layout Differences

Non-US keyboards may require different shortcuts. Check your OS settings for alternate key combinations.

Mobile App Glitches

Restart the app or device if the symbol fails to insert.

Final Tips for Power Users

  • Create a Shortcut Note: Save a note with frequently used symbols (like °, ±, µ) for quick copy-pasting.
  • Use Templates: If you often write temperature logs, create an Evernote template with predefined symbols.
  • Third-Party Tools: Apps like PopChar (Mac) or WinCompose (Windows) can simplify symbol insertion.

By mastering these techniques, you’ll ensure your notes are as precise and professional as the ideas they capture. Whether you’re tracking global temperatures or baking the perfect cake, the humble degree symbol is a small but mighty tool in your Evernote arsenal.
