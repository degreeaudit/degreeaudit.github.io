What Is a Degree in Kinesiology and Why Should You Pursue It?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

In today’s fast-paced world, health and wellness have taken center stage. From the rise of remote work leading to sedentary lifestyles to the growing awareness of mental health, people are increasingly looking for ways to optimize their physical and emotional well-being. This is where a degree in kinesiology comes into play. But what exactly is kinesiology, and why should you consider pursuing it?

Understanding Kinesiology

Kinesiology is the scientific study of human movement, performance, and function. It combines principles from anatomy, physiology, biomechanics, neuroscience, and psychology to understand how the body moves and how physical activity impacts overall health.

The Core Disciplines of Kinesiology

  1. Exercise Science – Focuses on how physical activity improves fitness, prevents disease, and enhances performance.
  2. Biomechanics – Examines the mechanics of movement, such as how muscles, bones, and joints work together.
  3. Sports Psychology – Studies the mental aspects of athletic performance, including motivation and stress management.
  4. Motor Control & Learning – Explores how the brain coordinates movement and how skills are acquired.
  5. Rehabilitation Sciences – Helps individuals recover from injuries or manage chronic conditions through movement therapy.

Why Pursue a Degree in Kinesiology?

1. Growing Demand in Health & Wellness Industries

With rising obesity rates, an aging population, and increased focus on preventive healthcare, professionals with a background in kinesiology are in high demand. Careers in personal training, physical therapy, occupational therapy, and corporate wellness programs are booming.

2. Versatile Career Opportunities

A kinesiology degree doesn’t lock you into one career path. Graduates can work in:
- Clinical Settings (e.g., physical therapy, chiropractic care)
- Sports & Athletics (e.g., strength and conditioning coaching)
- Corporate Wellness (e.g., designing workplace health programs)
- Research & Academia (e.g., studying movement disorders or sports performance)

3. Impact on Public Health

Chronic diseases like diabetes, heart disease, and hypertension are major global concerns. Kinesiology professionals play a crucial role in promoting active lifestyles, designing rehabilitation programs, and educating communities on the benefits of movement.

4. High Earning Potential

While salaries vary by specialization, many kinesiology-related careers offer competitive pay. For example:
- Physical Therapists – Median salary of $95,620/year (BLS, 2022)
- Exercise Physiologists – Median salary of $51,350/year (BLS, 2022)
- Sports Medicine Physicians – Can earn $200,000+ annually

5. Alignment with Future Trends

The fitness industry is evolving with wearable tech, virtual training, and AI-driven health analytics. A kinesiology degree equips you with the knowledge to adapt to these innovations and stay ahead in the field.

How to Choose the Right Kinesiology Program

Accreditation & Curriculum

Look for programs accredited by organizations like the American Kinesiology Association (AKA). A strong curriculum should include:
- Hands-on lab experience
- Internship opportunities
- Specializations in areas like sports medicine or clinical rehabilitation

Faculty & Research Opportunities

Programs with active research in biomechanics, sports performance, or rehabilitation science provide valuable learning experiences.

Networking & Career Support

Schools with strong ties to hospitals, sports teams, and wellness companies can help you secure internships and job placements.

Final Thoughts

If you’re passionate about health, movement, and helping others, a degree in kinesiology could be your gateway to a fulfilling career. Whether you want to work with athletes, rehabilitate patients, or influence public health policies, this field offers endless possibilities.

The world needs more experts who understand the science of movement—will you be one of them?
