Degree vs Secret: Which Deodorant is Best for Preventing Excessive Wetness?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Sweating is a natural bodily function, but excessive wetness can be uncomfortable and embarrassing—especially in today’s fast-paced world where personal hygiene is more scrutinized than ever. With rising global temperatures due to climate change and increasing stress levels from modern lifestyles, finding the right deodorant to combat sweat has become a necessity. Two of the biggest names in the antiperspirant game, Degree and Secret, have long competed for dominance. But which one truly delivers when it comes to preventing excessive wetness?

The Science Behind Sweat and Antiperspirants

Before diving into the Degree vs Secret debate, it’s important to understand how antiperspirants work. Unlike deodorants, which simply mask odor, antiperspirants contain aluminum-based compounds (like aluminum chloride or aluminum zirconium) that temporarily block sweat ducts, reducing moisture.

With heatwaves becoming more frequent—2023 was the hottest year on record—people are sweating more than ever. Add in the stress of remote work, economic instability, and social pressures, and it’s no wonder sweat control is a top priority.

Degree: Built for Extreme Conditions?

The "MotionSense" Technology

Degree markets itself as the deodorant for people on the move. Its MotionSense™ technology claims to release odor-fighting ingredients with movement, making it ideal for athletes or those with active lifestyles. But does it hold up against excessive wetness?

Pros:

  • Strong sweat protection (48-hour claims in some variants)
  • Designed for high-intensity activities
  • Gender-neutral options available

Cons:

  • Can feel sticky if overapplied
  • Some users report white residue on dark clothing

Given that fitness culture is booming (with over 60% of Americans now engaging in regular exercise), Degree’s athletic-focused branding gives it an edge. But what about those who need all-day protection without hitting the gym?

Secret: The Power of Clinical Strength

The "Stress Response" Factor

Secret has long positioned itself as a brand that understands women’s sweat concerns, but its clinical-strength formulas are now popular among all genders. Their Stress Response line is specifically designed to combat sweat triggered by anxiety—a major issue in today’s high-pressure society.

Pros:

  • Stronger aluminum concentrations in clinical variants
  • Lightweight, fast-absorbing formulas
  • More fragrance options for sensitive skin

Cons:

  • Some users experience irritation with prolonged use
  • Not all variants are as long-lasting as Degree’s

With mental health awareness at an all-time high, Secret’s focus on stress-induced sweating is a smart move. But does it outperform Degree in real-world conditions?

Real-World Testing: Which One Wins?

Under Arm Sweat Tests

In a 2023 independent study comparing Degree UltraClear Black + White and Secret Clinical Strength Soft Solid, both performed well—but Degree had a slight edge in longevity, especially during physical activity. Secret, however, was rated higher for comfort and skin feel.

Environmental Impact Considerations

With sustainability being a major concern, both brands have faced scrutiny. Degree’s parent company, Unilever, has pledged to reduce plastic waste, while Secret (owned by Procter & Gamble) has introduced recyclable packaging. Still, neither brand is fully eco-friendly yet.

Final Verdict: Which Should You Choose?

  • For athletes & heavy sweaters → Degree
  • For stress-related sweat & sensitive skin → Secret

Ultimately, the best choice depends on your lifestyle. As climate change and societal pressures continue to increase sweat triggers, both brands will need to innovate further. But for now, the battle between Degree and Secret remains a close one.
