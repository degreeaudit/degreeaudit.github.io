The Best YouTube Channels for Quantity Surveying Degree Students

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Quantity surveying is a dynamic field that blends finance, construction, and project management. For students pursuing a degree in this discipline, YouTube can be an invaluable resource for supplemental learning, industry insights, and career advice. With the global construction industry facing challenges like sustainability, digital transformation, and labor shortages, staying updated is more critical than ever. Below, we’ve curated a list of the best YouTube channels that every quantity surveying student should follow.

Why YouTube is Essential for Quantity Surveying Students

The construction industry is evolving rapidly, driven by technological advancements and global economic shifts. Traditional classroom learning alone may not cover emerging trends like Building Information Modeling (BIM), smart contracts, or green construction practices. YouTube offers:

  • Real-world case studies from industry professionals
  • Software tutorials (e.g., CostX, Revit, Bluebeam)
  • Career guidance from seasoned quantity surveyors
  • Global perspectives on construction economics

Here are the top YouTube channels to help you excel in your studies and future career.

1. The Quantity Surveyor

(Channel Focus: Career Development & Industry Trends)

This channel is a goldmine for students who want to understand the practical side of quantity surveying. The host, a practicing QS, breaks down complex topics like:

  • How to land your first job in quantity surveying
  • Salary expectations in different countries
  • The impact of AI on cost estimation

With the rise of automation, understanding how technology affects traditional QS roles is crucial. This channel provides actionable advice on staying relevant in a competitive job market.

2. BIM & Digital Construction

(Channel Focus: Technology in Construction)

BIM is revolutionizing the construction industry, and quantity surveyors must adapt. This channel covers:

  • How BIM integrates with cost management
  • Best practices for 5D BIM (cost estimation)
  • Case studies of digital transformation in mega-projects

Given the global push for sustainable construction, this channel also explores how digital tools can optimize resource efficiency.

3. Construction Management

(Channel Focus: Project Finance & Risk Management)

A strong grasp of financial principles is essential for QS professionals. This channel dives into:

  • Cost control strategies in volatile markets
  • The role of QS in mitigating inflation risks
  • Contract administration and dispute resolution

With supply chain disruptions affecting construction timelines worldwide, learning risk management from this channel is a must.

4. QS Tutor

(Channel Focus: Academic Support & Exam Prep)

Struggling with coursework? QS Tutor offers:

  • Step-by-step tutorials on measurement techniques (NRM, SMM7)
  • Sample take-off exercises
  • Tips for passing the RICS APC assessment

This channel is particularly useful for students who need extra help with technical calculations and standards.

5. The Green Building Expert

(Channel Focus: Sustainable Construction & Cost Implications)

Sustainability is no longer optional—it’s a necessity. This channel explores:

  • Life-cycle costing for eco-friendly materials
  • Government incentives for green projects
  • How QS professionals can drive sustainability in construction

With net-zero targets looming globally, understanding green finance is a career booster.

6. Global Construction Review

(Channel Focus: International Projects & Market Trends)

For students eyeing a global career, this channel covers:

  • Mega-projects in the Middle East and Asia
  • How geopolitical issues affect construction costs
  • Differences in QS practices across jurisdictions

Given the post-pandemic infrastructure boom, staying informed about global trends is key.

How to Maximize Learning from These Channels

  1. Create a study playlist – Organize videos by topic (e.g., BIM, contracts, sustainability).
  2. Engage with the community – Ask questions in the comments or join LinkedIn discussions.
  3. Apply knowledge practically – Use software tutorials to complete university assignments.

The construction industry is at a crossroads, with digitalization and sustainability reshaping traditional roles. By leveraging these YouTube channels, quantity surveying students can gain a competitive edge and prepare for the future of the profession.
