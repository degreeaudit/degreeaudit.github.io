The Best BS Degrees for Career Changers

Home / Blog / Blog Details

The modern workforce is more dynamic than ever, with career changes becoming the norm rather than the exception. Whether driven by technological disruption, economic shifts, or personal growth, professionals are increasingly seeking new paths to stay relevant. For career changers, earning a Bachelor of Science (BS) degree can be a strategic move—especially in fields with high demand, strong earning potential, and opportunities for advancement.

Here’s a deep dive into the best BS degrees for career changers, tailored to today’s most pressing global challenges.

1. Computer Science: The Backbone of the Digital Revolution

Why It’s a Smart Choice

The tech industry continues to dominate job growth, with roles in software development, cybersecurity, and artificial intelligence (AI) offering lucrative salaries and remote work flexibility. A BS in Computer Science provides foundational skills in programming, algorithms, and data structures—making it ideal for career changers looking to break into tech.

Key Career Paths

  • Software Developer (Median salary: $120,730)
  • Cybersecurity Analyst (Median salary: $112,000)
  • Data Scientist (Median salary: $131,490)

Who Should Consider This?

If you enjoy problem-solving, logic, and continuous learning, this degree offers a clear path to high-paying roles with strong job security.

2. Environmental Science: Tackling Climate Change Head-On

Why It’s a Smart Choice

With climate change driving policy shifts and corporate sustainability initiatives, professionals in environmental science are in high demand. A BS in Environmental Science equips career changers with expertise in conservation, renewable energy, and environmental policy—skills critical for a greener future.

Key Career Paths

  • Environmental Consultant (Median salary: $76,530)
  • Sustainability Specialist (Median salary: $73,230)
  • Renewable Energy Engineer (Median salary: $100,000+)

Who Should Consider This?

If you’re passionate about sustainability and want a career with purpose, this degree aligns with global efforts to combat climate change.

3. Nursing: A Recession-Proof Career in Healthcare

Why It’s a Smart Choice

Healthcare remains one of the most stable industries, with nursing shortages creating abundant job opportunities. A BS in Nursing (BSN) opens doors to roles in hospitals, telehealth, and specialized care—offering both job security and personal fulfillment.

Key Career Paths

  • Registered Nurse (RN) (Median salary: $81,220)
  • Nurse Practitioner (NP) (Median salary: $121,610)
  • Clinical Nurse Specialist (Median salary: $96,000)

Who Should Consider This?

If you thrive in fast-paced, people-centric roles and want a career with immediate impact, nursing is an excellent choice.

4. Business Analytics: Turning Data into Decisions

Why It’s a Smart Choice

Every industry relies on data to drive strategy, making business analytics a versatile and high-demand field. A BS in Business Analytics teaches statistical modeling, data visualization, and predictive analytics—skills that translate across finance, marketing, and operations.

Key Career Paths

  • Data Analyst (Median salary: $85,000)
  • Business Intelligence Analyst (Median salary: $90,000)
  • Operations Research Analyst (Median salary: $86,200)

Who Should Consider This?

If you enjoy working with numbers and want a degree that applies to nearly every sector, business analytics offers flexibility and growth potential.

5. Psychology: Understanding Human Behavior in a Digital Age

Why It’s a Smart Choice

Mental health awareness is at an all-time high, and organizations are prioritizing employee well-being. A BS in Psychology provides foundational knowledge in human behavior, which can lead to careers in HR, counseling, or market research.

Key Career Paths

  • Human Resources Specialist (Median salary: $64,240)
  • Market Research Analyst (Median salary: $68,230)
  • Substance Abuse Counselor (Median salary: $49,710)

Who Should Consider This?

If you’re empathetic and curious about human motivation, psychology offers diverse career options beyond clinical practice.

6. Information Technology (IT): Bridging the Gap Between Tech and Business

Why It’s a Smart Choice

IT professionals are essential in every industry, from healthcare to finance. A BS in IT focuses on networking, cloud computing, and systems administration—skills that are always in demand.

Key Career Paths

  • IT Support Specialist (Median salary: $57,910)
  • Cloud Solutions Architect (Median salary: $130,000)
  • Network Administrator (Median salary: $84,810)

Who Should Consider This?

If you prefer hands-on tech work without heavy coding, IT offers a practical entry point into the tech world.

7. Public Health: A Growing Field in a Post-Pandemic World

Why It’s a Smart Choice

The COVID-19 pandemic highlighted the critical need for public health professionals. A BS in Public Health prepares career changers for roles in epidemiology, health education, and policy—addressing global health disparities.

Key Career Paths

  • Epidemiologist (Median salary: $78,830)
  • Health Educator (Median salary: $56,500)
  • Public Health Analyst (Median salary: $70,000)

Who Should Consider This?

If you’re passionate about community health and disease prevention, this degree offers meaningful work with societal impact.

Final Thoughts on Choosing the Right BS Degree

The best BS degree for career changers depends on your interests, skills, and long-term goals. Whether you’re drawn to tech, healthcare, sustainability, or business, each of these degrees provides a solid foundation for a successful transition. The key is to align your education with industry trends and personal strengths—ensuring a rewarding and future-proof career.
